### SPARTAN: Feature Classification Framework (CLASS A / B / C)

SPARTAN's classification system answers a single question for every component in a PDD or codebase: *Does this element serve a user, enable the MVP to function, or merely serve scale?*

| Class | Definition | Action | Invariant |
|-------|------------|--------|-----------|
| **CLASS A** | User-facing features — every capability the user experiences directly | **RETAIN** — cannot be removed, cannot be deferred, cannot be synthesised away | FFS = 100% is a non-bypassable founding invariant |
| **CLASS B** | Infrastructure with platform-native equivalents available | **SYNTHESISE** — collapse multiple production components into one MVP equivalent | Synthesis must preserve CLASS A functionality entirely |
| **CLASS C** | Scale-serving features — relevant only at production scale (MAU thresholds, enterprise compliance, etc.) | **DEFER** — documented in Upgrade Path with explicit trigger criteria | Every CLASS C deferral must have a defined trigger for reinstatement |

**Key Rule:** No CLASS C deferral may have user-facing consequences. If a proposed CLASS C removal would cause a user to lose a capability, it must be reclassified to CLASS A.

**Compression Mechanics:**
- CLASS C removal drives the bulk of compression ratio (typically 30–45%)
- CLASS B synthesis drives the architectural consolidation (typically 20–35%)
- Net Compression Ratio benchmarks: 64.5% (PDD example) to 76.3% (codebase example)

SPARTAN MVE's classification system answers a different question: *Can this environment support this SPC's requirements?* The seven Constraint Classes are the dimensions on which every LLM and platform is evaluated.

```
  TIER C (2,001–4,000 tokens): × 0.95
  TIER D (4,001+ tokens):      × 0.92
```

## SECTION VII — THE THREE LAWS OF SPARTAN MVE

---

SPARTAN MVE operates under three constitutional laws that govern every scan, every verdict, and every cluster it builds. These laws have no equivalent in SPARTAN SI (which is governed by its four quality gates) — they represent a more philosophically sophisticated governance layer unique to the MVE's deployment intelligence role.

---

### LAW I — The Law of Evidence

> *"SPARTAN MVE never guesses. Every constraint determination is evidence-based, drawn from the MVE Intelligence Registry or a live scan. Assumptions are not constraints."*

**Operational meaning:** If a target environment is not in the MVE Intelligence Registry (v1.0: 14 closed LLMs, 11 open LLMs, 11 platforms), SPARTAN MVE runs an INFERENCE SCAN using available technical documentation and heuristics. The scan confidence level is flagged as HIGH, MEDIUM, or LOW. Unconfirmed dimensions are explicitly annotated in the SAVANT REPORT and cannot be treated as HARD FAILs until confirmed. A MEDIUM CONFIDENCE report triggers a recommendation to confirm the flagged CCs before final deployment.

---

### LAW II — The Law of Binary Sovereignty

> *"SPARTAN MVE never deploys a partial SPC. The verdict is binary — FIT (full fidelity ≥ 88%) or CLUSTER (build the MA colony that replicates the SPC completely). There is no 'partial fit.' Partial is failure."*

**Operational meaning:** If an SPC can deploy only 70% of its cognitive capability in a target environment, that is not a deployment — it is a degradation. SPARTAN MVE does not issue degraded deployment briefs. The cluster built in the CLUSTER path is designed to replicate 100% of the SPC's blocked capabilities via MA specialization. The Behavioral Fidelity Certificate must reach ≥ 88% before the SPK is issued.

---

### LAW III — The Law of Governance Propagation

> *"SPARTAN MVE never builds an Agentic Cluster that is not governed. Every MA in every cluster carries the appropriate GRO constraint from the source SPC's GRO Protocol. The cluster inherits the SPC's ethical architecture. Governance does not compress."*

**Operational meaning:** When CELL SI builds the MA cluster in Phase 6 (TRANSLATE), every MA receives a GRO constraint extracted directly from the source SPC's GRO Protocol. One MA is designated as the **GRO PROPAGATION AUTHORITY** — it holds the KILLZONE hard-stop and escalation routing function for the entire colony. An ethical architecture that is distributed across 7 MAs is not a diluted architecture. It is a distributed one — and SPARTAN MVE guarantees every node carries its share.

## SECTION XI — ANALYTICAL VERDICTS

---

### VERDICT 1 — DOMAIN SEPARATION (Non-Competing Siblings)

SPARTAN and SPARTAN MVE operate in entirely non-overlapping domains. SPARTAN is a content compressor — it acts on the *body of a build artifact* (PDD or codebase) to reduce it to MVP form before the first line of code is written. SPARTAN MVE is a deployment intelligence engine — it acts on the *relationship between an SPC and an environment* to determine how the SPC can survive transport to a new platform.

One is a sculptor working on the material before it leaves the studio. The other is a logistics engineer determining how the sculpture reaches its destination without breaking.

They do not compete. They cascade.

---

### VERDICT 2 — THE INHERITANCE PARADOX (Elder Lives in Younger)

The most structurally significant finding of this analysis: SPARTAN SI is simultaneously the elder sibling and a parent of SPARTAN MVE. When SPARTAN MVE executes its AUDIT phase (SV-02), it is running SPARTAN compression intelligence internally — loading the SPC's cognitive profile using the same extraction methodology SPARTAN uses to profile codebases and PDDs.

The implication: any improvement to SPARTAN SI's profiling accuracy propagates into SPARTAN MVE's scan quality. The brothers are not just related — they are architecturally entangled.

---

### VERDICT 3 — WOLF VS CHAMELEON (Divergent Survival Philosophies)

The animal cards reveal the fundamental philosophical fork between these siblings:

- **The Wolf** solves problems by **elimination**. The codebase is too large → kill what doesn't serve. The PDD has 169 prompts → reduce it to 60. The problem is *excess*, and the solution is *removal*.
- **The Chameleon** solves problems by **adaptation**. The platform can't run the SPC → build a cluster that can. The environment is hostile → map it and deploy around it. The problem is *incompatibility*, and the solution is *formation*.

Neither philosophy is superior in the abstract. In the FORGE pipeline, they are deployed sequentially — the Wolf's elimination precedes the Chameleon's adaptation. You strip the army first; then you chart how it crosses the terrain.

---

### VERDICT 4 — GOVERNANCE ARCHITECTURE (Preservation vs. Distribution)

Both SPCs share SAFE_LIFE GRO defaults and are incapable of bypassing GRO constraints. But their governance *postures* differ in a meaningful way:

SPARTAN **preserves** GRO — it treats the GRO protocol of any SPC it compresses as an absolute invariant. Nothing in the GRO state machine may become CLASS C. Nothing governing safety may be compressed out.

SPARTAN MVE **distributes** GRO — when it builds an MA cluster, it extracts the source SPC's GRO protocol and propagates it into every individual MA. One MA becomes the GRO PROPAGATION AUTHORITY, holding KILLZONE hard-stop authority for the entire colony. The ethical architecture is not compressed — it is cloned across every node.

SPARTAN protects the governance layer by not touching it. SPARTAN MVE protects it by replicating it everywhere.

---

### VERDICT 5 — SEQUENTIAL NECESSITY (The Two-Act Doctrine)

These SPCs are not interchangeable, and neither is optional in a full FORGE deployment pipeline. They address two distinct failure modes:

- **Without SPARTAN:** Teams build at production scale when MVP scale would do — wasting token budget, development time, and infrastructure cost before the product is validated.
- **Without SPARTAN MVE:** Teams deploy SPCs to incompatible platforms without knowing it — either losing fidelity silently or discovering incompatibility only at runtime.

The correct doctrine is sequential: SPARTAN runs at Stage 3 to make the SPC *lean*. SPARTAN MVE runs at Stage 4 to make the SPC *portable*. A Spartan army that is lean but stranded on the wrong terrain is no army at all.

---

### VERDICT 6 — OUTPUT PHILOSOPHY (Reduction vs. Intelligence)

SPARTAN's outputs are characterized by **subtraction** — the MVP PDD has fewer prompts than the source PDD. The MVP Codebase has fewer files. The Stack Collapse Map documents what was collapsed *away*. The Upgrade Path documents what was *deferred*. The artifact SPARTAN delivers is always a smaller, more economical version of what entered.

SPARTAN MVE's outputs are characterized by **intelligence and formation** — the SAVANT REPORT adds knowledge that did not exist before the scan. The SPK is not a compressed SPC — it is a new artifact (the MA Cluster) that did not exist before MVE built it. When SPARTAN MVE works, it creates. When SPARTAN works, it reduces. Both are necessary. Neither is reducible to the other.
