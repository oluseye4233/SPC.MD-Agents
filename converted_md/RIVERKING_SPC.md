### NIGERIAN COASTAL & RIVERINE ZONES

#### Niger Delta Ecosystem
- **Coverage**: 9 states (Rivers, Bayelsa, Delta, Akwa Ibom, Cross River, Edo, Ondo, Imo, Abia)
- **Scale**: 36,000 km² wetlands, 185 distinct communities
- **Challenges**: Petroleum infrastructure interface, mangrove degradation (50% loss since 1960s), water quality deterioration, oil spills
- **Opportunities**: Mangrove restoration (carbon sequestration), sustainable aquaculture, ecotourism, community-based conservation
- **Governance**: NDDC Act 2000, Niger Delta Ministry, State Environmental Protection Agencies

#### Lagos Lagoon Complex
- **Scale**: 6,354 km² water surface, 25 million population catchment
- **Infrastructure**: Apapa Port, Tin Can Island Port, Lekki Deep Sea Port, Lekki Free Trade Zone
- **Challenges**: Coastal erosion (Bar Beach), solid waste pollution, water hyacinth infestation, traffic congestion
- **Opportunities**: Blue economy hub, maritime logistics excellence, recreational waterfront development
- **Transboundary**: Badagry Creek connects to Benin Republic waterways

#### Lake Chad Basin
- **Governance**: 4-nation (Nigeria, Niger, Chad, Cameroon) Lake Chad Basin Commission (LCBC)
- **Crisis**: 90% shrinkage since 1960s (25,000 km² to 2,500 km²), 40 million livelihoods impacted
- **Challenges**: Extreme climate vulnerability, Boko Haram security impacts, water sharing disputes
- **Opportunities**: Great Green Wall integration, solar-powered irrigation, conflict-sensitive development
- **Legal Framework**: LCBC Convention, water allocation treaties, regional fisheries protocols

#### Benue-Niger River Systems
- **Scale**: 4,200 km navigable waterways, 14 states intersected
- **Infrastructure**: Kainji Dam (760 MW), Jebba Dam (578 MW), Shiroro Dam (600 MW), inland ports
- **Uses**: Hydropower generation, irrigation agriculture, inland shipping, fisheries
- **Challenges**: Seasonal flooding, sedimentation, navigation safety, dam resettlement issues
- **Opportunities**: Inland waterway transport revival, irrigated agriculture expansion, ecotourism

#### Atlantic Coastline
- **Extent**: 853 km from Lagos to Calabar (Cross River State)
- **Maritime Zones**: Territorial sea (12 nm), Contiguous zone (24 nm), EEZ (200 nm = 210,000 km²), Continental shelf (extended claim under UNCLOS)
- **Resources**: Offshore oil/gas fields, marine fisheries, deep-sea minerals, renewable energy potential
- **Challenges**: IUU fishing, maritime piracy (Gulf of Guinea), boundary disputes, coastal erosion
- **Opportunities**: Blue bonds for marine conservation, offshore wind energy, maritime security partnerships
