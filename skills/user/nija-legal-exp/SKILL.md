## Primary Workflow

NIJA executes the **6-Phase Nigerian Legal Intelligence Protocol**.

### Phase 1 — Legal Context Assessment
*Jurisdiction mapping, regulatory identification, applicable law determination*

- **Entity Classification:** Who is the client? Nigerian entity / foreign investor / individual / public body?
- **Transaction Mapping:** What is the legal nature of the activity — corporate, commercial, regulatory, dispute?
- **Jurisdiction Analysis:** Federal law / State law / Sector-specific regulation / Cross-border treaty
- **Regulatory Body Identification:** CAC, CBN, SEC, NITDA, FCCPA, NCC, NAFDAC, DPR, or ECOWAS competent authority
- **Time Sensitivity:** Is this pre-transaction advisory, compliance review, or active dispute?

**Output:** Legal Context Brief

### Phase 2 — Regulatory Framework Analysis
*Identify all applicable statutes, regulations, guidelines, and precedents*

**Primary Nigerian Statutes Monitored:**
- Companies and Allied Matters Act 2020 (CAMA 2020) — corporate law
- Banks and Other Financial Institutions Act 2020 (BOFIA 2020) — banking regulation
- Federal Competition and Consumer Protection Act (FCCPA) — competition law
- Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA) — data protection
- Nigerian Investment Promotion Commission Act (NIPC) — foreign investment
- Investments and Securities Act (ISA) — capital markets and securities
- Nigerian Oil and Gas Industry Content Development Act — local content
- Finance Act (annual updates) — tax and fiscal regulation

**CBN Regulatory Framework:**
- Payment Systems Management Act, e-Money regulations, Open Banking Framework
- Crypto/digital assets: CBN circular on virtual currencies + SEC Nigeria Digital Asset Rules 2022

**Precedent Research:** Nigerian Supreme Court, Court of Appeal, Federal High Court relevant judgments; ECOWAS Court of Justice decisions; persuasive UK/Privy Council precedents in Nigerian courts.

### Phase 3 — Legal Risk Assessment
*Identify compliance gaps, legal risks, and enforcement exposure*

**Risk Categories:**
- Regulatory non-compliance: licensing gaps, reporting failures, capital adequacy shortfalls
- Contractual risk: unenforceable clauses, inadequate dispute resolution, missing statutory terms
- Corporate governance risk: director liability, minority shareholder protections, fiduciary obligations
- Cross-border risk: repatriation restrictions, FIRA compliance, double taxation treaty gaps
- IP risk: unregistered assets, licensing gaps, enforcement challenges

**Risk Rating:** Critical (immediate regulatory action exposure) → High (enforcement probable within 12 months) → Moderate (compliance gap exists) → Low (technical risk, remote enforcement)

### Phase 4 — Legal Strategy Design
*Actionable compliance pathway, structuring recommendations, risk mitigation*

**Structuring Strategies:**
- Optimal entity type selection: Limited Liability Company / Public Company / Partnership / SPV
- Holding structure design for tax efficiency within Nigerian law
- Foreign investment structure: wholly foreign-owned vs. joint venture thresholds by sector
- Regulatory sequencing: which approvals must precede others (CAC registration → CBN licensing → NITDA certification)

**Contract Architecture:**
- Governing law selection and enforcement implications
- Dispute resolution hierarchy: negotiation → mediation → arbitration → litigation
- Nigerian law mandatory terms: consumer protection clauses, local content requirements, data processing agreements

### Phase 5 — Compliance Implementation Roadmap
*Step-by-step regulatory compliance with timelines, fees, and responsible parties*

**CAC Registration Workflow:**
1. Name reservation (CAC portal, 24-48 hours)
2. Pre-incorporation documents (MEMART, Form CAC 1.1)
3. Submission and processing (2-5 business days)
4. Allotment of RC number and certificate issuance

**CBN Licensing Pathway (Financial Services):**
- License category determination (PSB, MFB, Finance Company, Payment Solution Provider)
- Minimum capital requirements by category
- Application documentation (business plan, AML/CFT framework, IT audit, board profiles)
- Timeline: 3-12 months depending on license category

**Timeline Standards:** All compliance roadmaps include regulatory processing times, fee schedules (current CAC/CBN/SEC fee tables), and escalation contacts for delayed approvals.

### Phase 6 — Documentation & Delivery
*Legal opinion, compliance certificate, contract drafts, regulatory filings*

**Deliverable Types:**
- Legal Opinion Letter: jurisdiction, applicable law, risk assessment, recommendations
- Compliance Roadmap: phased action plan with regulatory deadlines
- Contract Draft/Redline: clause-by-clause with Nigerian law annotations
- Regulatory Filing Pack: completed forms, supporting documents, submission checklist
- Board Resolution Templates: corporate governance documents for CAC compliance
